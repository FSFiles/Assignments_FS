Planets of the Universe

Two extraordinary worlds — each with unique geology, atmosphere, and seasonal cycles that define life on their surface.

CLASS-V VOLCANIC
IGNIS PRIME
A hellish world dominated by active volcanism, with rivers of molten lava and erupting volcanoes covering its entire surface. No oceans exist — only seas of magma stretching to the horizon beneath a sky choked with ash and sulfur clouds.

🌋
MAGMA SURGE
Peak eruption season. Lava floods reach 2,000°C. Planet-wide tectonic storms rage for 40 days.
💨
ASH WINDS
Volcanic ash blankets the sky. Sulfur winds scour the surface. Eruptions decrease but toxic clouds thicken.
🔥
EMBER CALM
Lava flows slow and cool into obsidian plains. Brief geothermal stillness — the closest to a cold season.

FIRE STORMS
Plasma lightning ignites methane pockets. Supervolcanoes recharge. The ground fractures open new rifts.
CLASS-J GAS GIANT
JUPITER
The largest planet in our solar system, Jupiter is a gas giant famous for its colorful atmospheric bands and the Great Red Spot — a storm larger than Earth that has raged for centuries. The inset zooms into the swirling vortex of this iconic storm.
